The Legal Practice Area

Legal practice areas encompass specific areas of law that lawyers specialize in, such as business law, criminal law, family law, immigration law, and personal injury law, among others

Business Law

Business law is the legal framework that governs the creation, operation, and dissolution of businesses, covering contracts, employment, and liability.

Criminal Law

Criminal law is the body of law that deals with crimes and their punishment, including offenses against individuals and society.

Child Support

Child support law mandates that non-custodial parents financially support their children, ensuring their basic needs are met.

Education Law

Education law governs the operation of schools, including issues such as student rights, teacher employment, and curriculum development.

Divorce Law

Divorce law regulates the process of dissolving marriages, including issues such as property division, child custody, and spousal support.

Tax Law

Tax law governs the assessment and collection of taxes, including income, property, sales, and corporate taxes, among others.
